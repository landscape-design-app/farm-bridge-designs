## STRUCTURAL ADEQUACY OF 2' DEPTH

### 1. BEARING CAPACITY ✓ PASS

**Bearing Stress on 12"×12" Steel Plates**:
- Plate area: 144 square inches
- Maximum load: 5,000 lbs per plate (half of 10,000 lb total)
- Bearing stress: 5,000 ÷ 144 = **35 PSI**
- Concrete strength: 3,000 PSI minimum
- **Safety Factor: 85:1** (massively over-designed)

**Bearing Stress on Soil (through abutment base)**:
- Base area: 8' × 2' = 16 SF = 2,304 sq inches
- Total weight (structure + loads): 
  - Concrete abutment: ~4,000 lbs (estimated)
  - Applied loads: 10,000 lbs
  - Total: 14,000 lbs
- Soil bearing pressure: 14,000 ÷ 2,304 = **6.1 PSI** (875 PSF)
- Typical soil capacity: 1,500-3,000 PSF
- **Safety Factor: 2-3×** ✓

### 2. OVERTURNING STABILITY ✓ PASS

**Forces Acting on Abutment**:
- Stabilizing weight: 4,000 lbs × 1' moment arm = 4,000 ft-lbs
- Live load: 10,000 lbs × 0.67' moment arm = 6,700 ft-lbs
- Total resisting moment: **10,700 ft-lbs**

**Overturning Forces** (lateral earth pressure):
- Active earth pressure: 30 PCF × 3' height × 8' length = 720 lbs
- Moment arm: 1' (1/3 of height)
- Overturning moment: **720 ft-lbs**

**Safety Factor Against Overturning**:
- Ratio: 10,700 ÷ 720 = **14.9**
- Required minimum: 1.5
- **PASS with huge margin** ✓

### 3. SLIDING RESISTANCE ✓ PASS

**Resisting Forces**:
- Weight on base: 14,000 lbs
- Coefficient of friction (concrete on compacted AB3): 0.45
- Friction force: 14,000 × 0.45 = **6,300 lbs**
- PLUS: Ground anchors (6 × fiberglass rebar)

**Driving Forces**:
- Lateral earth pressure: 720 lbs
- Impact/seismic (estimated): 1,000 lbs
- Total: **1,720 lbs**

**Safety Factor Against Sliding**:
- Ratio: 6,300 ÷ 1,720 = **3.7**
- Required minimum: 1.5
- **PASS** ✓

### 4. STRUCTURAL MOMENT CAPACITY ✓ PASS

**Concrete Section Properties** (main wall):
- Depth: 2' (24 inches)
- Thickness: 2' (24 inches)  
- Rebar: #4 bars @ 12" o.c. both faces

**Applied Moment** (cantilever from base):
- Earth pressure: 720 lbs at 1' from base
- Applied moment: **720 ft-lbs = 8,640 in-lbs**

**Section Capacity**:
- Simplified analysis for #4 rebar:
- Moment capacity ≈ 15,000 in-lbs per foot of wall
- Over 8' length: **120,000 in-lbs total**

**Safety Factor**:
- Ratio: 120,000 ÷ 8,640 = **13.9**
- **Massive over-capacity** ✓

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### Q: Is 2' depth really strong enough?
**A**: Yes! Your safety factors are 3-15× the minimum requirements. The structure is over-designed even at 2' depth because your loading is so light compared to typical highway bridges.

### Q: What if I want to drive something heavier?
**A**: The design supports up to 10,000 lbs total load. That's about a 6,000 lb vehicle (small pickup truck). Anything heavier, consult an engineer.

### Q: Can I reduce it to 1.5' depth?
**A**: Not recommended. At 1.5', the safety factors drop to marginal levels. 2' is the minimum for comfortable margin.

### Q: Do I really need the ground anchors?
**A**: YES. They're critical for seismic zones and provide uplift resistance. They only cost ~$60 and take 1 hour to install. Don't skip them.

### Q: What about frost heave?
**A**: If you're in a frost zone, dig base below frost line OR use frost-protected shallow foundation techniques. Consult local building codes for frost depth in your area.

### Q: Can I use this design for a different span?
**A**: This abutment design is specifically for 24' span with light loading. For different spans or heavier loads, the design needs to be recalculated.
